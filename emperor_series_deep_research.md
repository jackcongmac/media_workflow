# Emperor Series Deep Research Brief

Date: 2026-06-09

## Research Question

Should we make a short-video series about emperors, power, and historical decisions? If yes, what format and visual style should be tested first?

## Short Answer

Yes, but do not position it as "history education."

Position it as:

**historical power micro-drama**

The best first experiment is not pure documentary, not pure fantasy, and not static art. It should be a hybrid:

**cinematic moving historical video + crisis-room narration + short-drama cliffhangers.**

Non-negotiable production rule:

The final output must feel like a short film, not a moving photo album. Still images can be used for covers, first-frame references, maps, or 1-2 second inserts, but the main body must be moving video.

## Why The Topic Has Potential

### 1. Vertical micro-drama is a proven attention format

Microdramas are built for fast mobile viewing, emotional escalation, and cliffhangers. Current reporting and market summaries show the category has grown from a China-led format into a global mobile entertainment business.

Useful signal:

- Microdrama apps recorded hundreds of millions of global downloads by 2025.
- International microdrama revenue outside China reached about US$1.4B in 2024 and is forecast to grow sharply.
- Popular genres include romance, revenge, identity reveal, supernatural/fantasy, and family drama.
- The core lesson is not "make romance"; it is "use high-conflict serialized storytelling."

Source:

- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microdrama
- https://www.businessinsider.com/micro-dramas-reelshort-dramabox-billion-dollar-business-in-us-2025-9
- https://www.businessinsider.com/peacock-reelshort-micro-dramas-bravo-hollywood-short-form-vertical-video-2026-5

### 2. AI historical reenactment is emerging

AI-generated historical vloggers have recently gained attention on YouTube and Instagram. The strongest examples use a character-led format instead of a textbook format: the audience follows a person inside the historical world.

Useful signal:

- The format makes history visual, personal, and immersive.
- Viewers tolerate AI when it has a clear creative purpose.
- The big risk is being labeled low-effort AI content if the output looks generic or historically wrong.

Source:

- https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/may/26/we-can-stitch-together-our-past-the-ai-generated-time-travellers-vlogging-from-history

### 3. 古装 is not a dead genre

Long-form Chinese costume drama continues to receive platform investment. 2025 examples include major historical/costume dramas such as `国色芳华`, which performed strongly across TV, streaming, and overseas distribution.

Useful signal:

- Audiences still respond to ancient settings, costumes, court politics, romance, fantasy, and visual spectacle.
- The stronger opportunity is not "正史讲解", but "古装情绪 + 权力冲突 + decision drama."

Source:

- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flourished_Peony
- https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/中国大陆电视剧列表_(2025年)
- https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/中国大陆电视剧列表_(2026年)

### 4. Shorts platforms reward entertainment more than education

Academic work on YouTube Shorts found Shorts are heavily entertainment-oriented and do not outperform regular videos as strongly in education/politics categories. Another study on Shorts recommendations found drift toward entertaining, joyful, or neutral content.

Implication:

History videos need entertainment packaging. The content can be researched, but the surface must be dramatic.

Source:

- https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.00454
- https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.04605

## Why The Topic Is Risky

### 1. Pure historical explanation is probably too slow

"今天讲秦始皇为什么焚书" is likely too lecture-like.

Better:

"如果你是秦始皇，天下刚统一，所有读书人还在用旧制度反驳你，你会让他们继续说吗？"

### 2. AI realism can trigger distrust

Both YouTube and TikTok require labeling realistic AI-generated or meaningfully AI-edited content. YouTube also warns that mass-produced, repetitive, generic AI content can become inauthentic content and lose monetization eligibility.

Implication:

The series must have visible authorship:

- clear narrative point of view
- source notes
- distinctive visual system
- no fake documentary claims
- AI disclosure

Sources:

- https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/14328491
- https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/1311392
- https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/creating-videos/ai-generated-content

### 3. Historical accuracy matters more in this niche

AI history content often fails through anachronisms: modern objects, wrong clothing, fake props, or invented facts.

Implication:

Every episode needs a small "historical QA" step before publishing.

## Audience Hypothesis

Primary audience:

- Chinese-speaking viewers who like history, power, palace politics, and dramatic decisions.
- Overseas Chinese or China-curious viewers who like cultural/history content but do not want long lectures.
- Male-skewing viewers may respond to war, empire, strategy, betrayal, and decision-making.
- Female-skewing viewers may respond more to palace tension, relationships, betrayal, identity, and moral choices.

The series should not choose only one side yet. Season 1 should test both:

- **Power/strategy episodes**: 秦始皇、刘邦、项羽、司马懿、李世民。
- **Emotion/palace episodes**: 武则天、汉哀帝、赵飞燕、崇祯、慈禧 or 唐宫权斗.

## Visual Style Options

### Option A: Cinematic Realistic Historical Style

Description:

AI-generated moving clips that look like high-budget historical drama shots. Camera movement, actor movement, robe movement, torch/fire/smoke motion, realistic lighting, no text inside video.

Pros:

- Highest short-video click potential.
- Feels closest to real drama and microdrama.
- Works well for covers.
- Compatible with future image-to-video clips.

Cons:

- More likely to trigger "AI slop" if faces look generic.
- Historical costume errors are obvious.
- Character consistency can drift.
- Requires AI disclosure.

Best use:

First 10-video test.

### Option B: 国画 / 水墨 Style

Description:

Animated ink wash, Chinese scroll painting, restrained color, symbolic scenes, moving brush texture, drifting mist, animated figures or silhouettes.

Pros:

- More culturally elegant.
- Less uncanny face risk.
- Less pressure to be photorealistic.
- Good for poetic, high-end brand identity.

Cons:

- Lower immediate drama/click energy.
- Harder to show facial conflict.
- Can feel educational or slow.
- Platform thumbnails may be less punchy.

Best use:

Special episodes, quote videos, dynasty overview, or reflective endings.

### Option C: 连环画 / 旧画报 Style

Description:

Animated Chinese vintage illustrated panels, bold line work, strong composition, subtitle-friendly, but with real motion: character turns, camera parallax, fire, flags, rain, doors opening, crowd movement.

Pros:

- Very distinctive.
- Easier to serialize.
- Lower realism burden.
- Naturally fits "episode panel" storytelling.
- Could become a recognizable channel style.

Cons:

- May look niche or old-fashioned if not modernized.
- Less premium than cinematic realism.
- Needs strong typography and motion design.

Best use:

Second test batch if realistic style feels too generic.

### Option D: Full AI Video / 真人式 Moving Drama

Description:

Image-to-video or text-to-video scenes with moving characters.

Pros:

- Highest immersion when it works.
- Strongest drama feeling.

Cons:

- Expensive.
- Slow.
- Continuity problems.
- Wrong hands, costumes, lips, and action can waste time.

Best use:

This is now the primary production direction, but it should be made shot-by-shot rather than as one long generated clip.

## Recommended Style Decision

Start with:

**80% cinematic moving historical clips + 10% maps/objects/documents + 10% animated ink/illustration transition cards.**

Do not start with static image slideshow.

Reason:

- Moving realistic shots give drama and cover appeal.
- Maps/objects create factual credibility.
- Ink/illustration cards give Chinese cultural identity.
- Short generated clips are more controllable than trying to make one continuous 60-90 second AI video.

## Minimum Validation Test

Before making a 10-video season, make 3 videos from the same script:

1. Realistic moving drama style.
2. Animated 国画 style.
3. Animated 连环画 style.

Same title. Same voice. Same duration. Same publish copy.

Publish as unlisted/private previews first, then share with 10-20 people or post one by one.

Measure:

- First 3-second retention.
- Completion rate.
- Comments.
- Which thumbnail people click.
- Which style people describe as "想看下一集".

Decision rule:

- If realistic gets better clicks and completion, use it for Season 1.
- If 连环画 gets stronger comments and identity recognition, build the channel around it.
- If 国画 gets praise but weak retention, use it only for covers/transitions.

## Suggested First 3 Test Topics

### Test 1: 秦始皇 - 焚书前夜

Why:

Famous, morally controversial, easy hook.

Question:

统一天下以后，还允许思想不统一吗？

### Test 2: 李世民 - 玄武门前夜

Why:

Family, power, survival, betrayal. Strong drama.

Question:

如果不先杀兄弟，明天死的会不会是你？

### Test 3: 崇祯 - 最后一场朝会

Why:

Emotional collapse, end-of-dynasty tragedy, strong visual mood.

Question:

一个皇帝有没有可能亲眼看着国家沉下去，却没有一个可用的人？

## Current Recommendation

Proceed with a 3-style pilot, not a full season.

Make one script, three visual treatments:

- realistic
- ink/国画
- 连环画

The winner becomes the Season 1 visual standard.
